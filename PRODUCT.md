# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Dominican Republic property managers handling multiple rental properties, tenants, and lease agreements. They use this app daily at a desk — it's their primary operational tool for tracking occupancy, contracts, and rent collection. They need to move fast through data-heavy screens, spot problems (overdue payments, expiring contracts), and take action quickly. Mix of office use and occasional mobile check-ins when visiting properties.

## Product Purpose

Centralized property management for Dominican Republic rental operators: contracts, payments, maintenance, and occupancy tracking in one place. Free and open-source under the GNU license, built to be accessible and practical for independent landlords and small management firms who can't afford enterprise platforms.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, capable, warm. This is a tool that feels like a trusted local partner, not a cold corporate platform, not a flashy startup. It knows the Dominican market, speaks the language, and respects the user's time. Professional without being sterile. Open-source ethos: transparent, community-driven, no vendor lock-in.

## Anti-references

- NOT a Silicon Valley SaaS (no purple gradients, no glassmorphism)
- NOT a bank (no navy-and-gold stuffiness)
- NOT a beach resort website (no sunset gradients, no palm tree illustrations)
- NOT generic admin template (no identical card grids with colored left borders)
- NOT enterprise software that looks like it costs $50k/year

## Design Principles

1. **Data density with clarity** — Property managers work with tables, numbers, and statuses all day. Pack information efficiently but maintain breathing room through smart spacing hierarchy.
2. **Status at a glance** — Use color meaningfully for states (available/occupied/maintenance, paid/pending/overdue). Every status should be instantly scannable without reading text.
3. **Warm professionalism** — Tint every surface toward warmth. No cold grays. Neutrals should feel like sand and stone, not steel and concrete.
4. **Action-oriented** — The most important actions should be obvious. Progressive disclosure for secondary operations. Don't hide functionality, but don't overwhelm either.
5. **Caribbean confidence** — The design should feel like it belongs in the Dominican Republic. Local, grounded, not imported. Typography and color should reflect tropical sophistication, not generic internationalism.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Optimized for adults of all ages. Easy-to-use tooling with clear affordances and generous touch targets. WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as baseline. Considerations:

- High contrast ratios for text readability in bright office environments
- Large, clearly labeled interactive elements for users less comfortable with technology
- Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Color never used as the sole indicator of state (always paired with text or icons)
- Keyboard navigable for power users
- Spanish-first UI with clear, plain language
